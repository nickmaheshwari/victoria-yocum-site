About Me

I am a research technician with the UNMH Focus early-intervention program, and a mentor/tutor with New Mexico Simons Scholars, a college enrichment program. I am very highly motivated, dedicated, and responsible and am always open to gaining new knowledge or experience.

Office of the Medical Investigator - New Mexico

Morphology Technician• February 2020 - June 2021 • http://www.hsc.unm.edu/omi/.com

The Office of the Medical Investigator (OMI) directs all death-related investigative activities in the State of New Mexico, examining any death that is sudden, violent, untimely, unexpected, or where a person is found dead and the cause of death is unknown. As a special program of the University of New Mexico School of Medicine, Department of Pathology, the OMI also provides advanced forensic pathology instruction to residents, fellows, and numerous University classes related to forensic investigation and medical education. The Radiology-Pathology Center for Forensic Imaging (CFI), at the UNM School of Medicine is located within the OMI, and is currently the only forensic center in the United States with in-house computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) facilities, which support forensic research and education, and the clinical service of the OMI. As a result of this all autopsy services are conducted in Albuquerque in a state-of-the-art facility, which is considered to be the best in the country. I began working at OMI during my undergraduate studies, where I was a Student Morphology Technician. This involved me assisting with the conduct of the autopsy, preparing and completing autopsy paperwork/data entry, supporting the maintenance of the lab by ensuring equipment/supplies are stocked, and familiarizing myself with autopsy chemicals, instruments and equipment.

After 3 months working as a Student Morphology Technician, my role was expanded and I quickly transitioned into a full-time Morphology Technician. I was responsible for conducting the prosection and evisceration process and assisting in training resident physicians in autopsy procedures. I provided technical morphology lab services for hospital, forensic, or anatomical autopsy cases, and general support to pathologists and other technical staff. Additionally, I performed sensitive computer data entry, and prepared applicable reports and autopsy paperwork for each case. I became proficient in practicing safety and environmental/infectious control methods, including becoming acquainted with COVID-19 safety procedures. Through this role, I have acquired vast hands-on knowledge of anatomy and pathology, and a familiarity working in a high-stress, fast-paced environment.

University of New Mexico FOCUS Program

Pediatric Early Intervention• June 2017 - September 2017 • https://unmhealth.org/services/development-disabilities/programs/early-childhood-programs/echfs/focus.html

The FOCUS Early Intervention Program provides specialized supports and services for families of children aged birth to three years old, specifically children who have developmental delay or are at risk of experiencing delay due to family substance use, family violence, trauma, prenatal exposure to substances, etc. FOCUS strives to improve family well-being by strengthening family capacity to support resilience and protective behaviors for their children, providing culturally sensitive service strategies, collaborating with the community, and maximizing access to quality services. Through my research on young infants who experience prenatal exposure to medications for opioid-use disorder, I had the opportunity to intern with the program. I worked closely with the Early Intervention specialists to provide general medical care in a clinical setting, as well as social work services through home-visits. Additionally, I worked with the UNM ADOBE Program, which provides wrap-around services for youths that have been incarcerated in the Bernalillo County Youth Services Center(YSC), and with the UNM Milagro Program, New Mexico's first comprehensive prenatal care program for pregnant women with substance use disorders. This also allowed me to shadow in obstetrics and family medicine practices. The internship provided me with vast knowledge on early-intervention work, and substance-use disorders. It changed my view on addiction, trauma, and mental health, and ultimately inspired me to continue to make an impact in this community.

Publications

Cognitive Outcomes of Young Children...

Abstract

Importance The number of children with prenatal opioid exposure to medication for addiction treatment (MAT) with methadone and buprenorphine for maternal opioid use disorder is increasing, but the associations of this exposure with cognitive outcomes are not well understood.

Objective To examine the strength and consistency of findings in the medical literature regarding the association of prenatal exposure to MAT with early childhood cognitive development, particularly when accounting for variables outside MAT exposure.

Data Sources A search strategy obtained publications from PubMed, CINAHL, PsycINFO, Web of Science, and Embase from January 1972 to June 2019. Reference lists from identified articles were searched.

Study Selection Inclusion criteria were cohort studies, studies including children aged 1 to 60 months with at least 2 months of prenatal MAT exposure, studies using standardized direct-observation testing scales, and studies reporting means and SDs. Case reports, case series, historical controls, and reviews were excluded.
